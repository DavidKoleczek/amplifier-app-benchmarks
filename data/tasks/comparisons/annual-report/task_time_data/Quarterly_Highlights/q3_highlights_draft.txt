Q3 2025 HIGHLIGHTS DRAFT
========================
Department Quarterly Review - July through September

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
-----------------
A quarter of significant transition. Company-wide reorganization announced in
August impacted team structure and morale. Despite challenges, delivered on
critical commitments including SOC2 certification and Mobile App launch. 
Customer satisfaction dipped mid-quarter but stabilized by end of September.

THE REORGANIZATION
------------------
On August 15, company announced restructuring affecting all departments.
For our team:
- Headcount reduced from 53 to 45 (-15%)
- Contractor positions eliminated (4 technical writers)
- Two positions consolidated
- All reductions handled with dignity and support
- Severance and outplacement provided

Team showed remarkable resilience. No critical capabilities lost. Maintained
all customer commitments despite transition.

KEY ACCOMPLISHMENTS
-------------------

1. SOC2 Type II Certification - ACHIEVED
   - Certification received September 28
   - Clean audit with no exceptions
   - Enabled 4 enterprise deals in pipeline
   - Major competitive differentiator established

2. Mobile App v3.0 - LAUNCHED (with delay)
   - GA release August 15 (2 weeks late)
   - Delay due to team transition, not technical issues
   - 4.8 star rating achieved
   - 45% increase in mobile engagement

3. Customer Analytics Dashboard - CONTINUED
   - Development continued despite disruption
   - Team prioritized effectively
   - Some features deferred to Q4
   - Core functionality on track

4. Disaster Recovery Enhancement - STARTED
   - Critical project initiated post-reorg
   - Leadership recognized importance of resilience
   - RTO improvement work underway

METRICS SNAPSHOT
----------------
- Customer Satisfaction: 84 → 77 → 80 (dip and recovery)
- NPS: 45 → 31 → 36 (similar pattern)
- Project Delivery: 83% on-time (1 delay)
- Budget: 16% under plan (reorg savings)
- Support Response Time: peaked at 5.2 hours, recovered to 3.8

CHALLENGES (Candid Assessment)
------------------------------
- Morale significantly impacted by reorg
- Knowledge transfer gaps from departures
- Customer concerns about stability
- Workload redistribution took time
- Communication fatigue

WHAT WENT WELL DESPITE EVERYTHING
---------------------------------
- Zero customer churn during transition
- SOC2 achieved on original timeline
- Mobile launch happened (delayed but successful)
- Team pulled together remarkably
- Transparent communication maintained trust

RECOGNITION
-----------
- Entire Department: Resilience Award from CEO
- Operations Team: kept everything running
- Customer Success: proactive customer outreach prevented churn

LESSONS LEARNED
---------------
- Over-communication during change is never too much
- Cross-training investments paid off when people left
- Customer relationships matter most during uncertainty
- Team culture is a competitive advantage
