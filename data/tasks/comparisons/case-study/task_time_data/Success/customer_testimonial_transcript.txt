HEALTHFIRST CASE STUDY - INTERVIEW TRANSCRIPT
Date: November 18, 2025
Interviewer: Marcus Thompson, CloudSync Marketing
Participants: Michael Chen (CIO), Dr. Amanda Torres (Chief of Emergency Medicine)

---

[INTRODUCTIONS - 0:00-2:15]

MARCUS: Thank you both for taking the time today. Before we start, I want to confirm that you've both reviewed and approved the consent form for this case study. We'll be recording this interview and may use quotes in marketing materials.

MICHAEL: Yes, all signed and approved through our legal and communications teams.

AMANDA: Same here. Happy to share our experience.

---

[BACKGROUND - 2:15-8:30]

MARCUS: Let's start with some background. Michael, can you tell me about HealthFirst and what drove your decision to look for a new file management solution?

MICHAEL: Sure. HealthFirst Regional Medical Center is a 450-bed acute care hospital in the suburbs of Philadelphia. We have about 3,200 employees, including 2,400 who needed access to our file systems. We serve a diverse community and handle about 65,000 ER visits a year.

Our previous setup was... let's call it "evolved organically." We had six different file servers accumulated over 15 years. Different departments had their own systems. Some clinical documents were in one place, admin docs in another. Radiology had their own setup. It was a mess, frankly.

MARCUS: What was the breaking point that made you decide to change?

MICHAEL: Two things happened close together. First, we had a HIPAA audit that went okay but identified some gaps in our document access controls and audit logging. Nothing that resulted in fines, but we got put on notice that we needed to improve.

Second, we had an incident where a clinician couldn't find a critical document during a patient emergency. The document existed - it just took too long to locate. No patient harm, thankfully, but it was a wake-up call.

AMANDA: I remember that incident. It was in my department. The frustration level was already high with the old systems, but that moment really crystallized the risk. When you're treating a trauma patient, you can't spend two minutes searching for their advance directive.

---

[VENDOR SELECTION - 8:30-14:15]

MARCUS: How did you evaluate vendors?

MICHAEL: We looked at five solutions, including the big enterprise suites and some healthcare-specific options. CloudSync stood out for a few reasons.

First, the compliance capabilities. The HIPAA template was exactly what our compliance team needed - not just checkboxes, but actual automated controls.

Second, the Epic integration. We're an Epic shop, and having tight integration with our EMR was critical. Some vendors treated that as an afterthought.

Third, honestly, the people. Your sales team - I think Janet was our rep - she brought in technical resources who actually understood healthcare. They didn't just show us generic demos.

AMANDA: I wasn't involved in the selection process directly, but I remember Michael asking me about what clinical workflows needed. That was different - usually IT just picks something and tells us to use it.

---

[IMPLEMENTATION EXPERIENCE - 14:15-28:00]

MARCUS: Let's talk about the implementation. I understand it wasn't entirely smooth sailing at first?

MICHAEL: [laughs] That's a diplomatic way to put it. No, the implementation took longer than originally planned - about five weeks longer. And the go-live weekend was... challenging.

MARCUS: Can you tell me more about that?

MICHAEL: Look, I believe in being honest about these things. The first 48 hours after go-live were rough. We had performance issues, some login problems, and our Epic integration wasn't working right. The Emergency Department was particularly impacted, and Amanda was not happy with me.

AMANDA: That's putting it mildly. I was in Michael's office on Saturday afternoon telling him this was affecting patient care. I was seriously questioning whether we'd made the right choice.

MARCUS: What happened next?

MICHAEL: CloudSync's response is actually what turned things around. Within hours, we had their VP on the phone apologizing and committing resources. Engineers worked through the weekend. By Monday, most issues were resolved. By Wednesday, we were stable.

But here's what really mattered - they were honest with us. They didn't make excuses or blame our environment. They said "we have a problem, here's what we're doing about it, here's when it will be fixed." That transparency meant a lot.

AMANDA: I'll admit, I was ready to send some strongly worded emails that Saturday. But Michael asked me to give it a few more days, and the CloudSync team really did step up. They extended their support period, they brought in extra people, and they fixed everything they said they would.

MARCUS: Amanda, you mentioned you were skeptical. What changed your mind?

AMANDA: A few things. First, the problems got fixed quickly. But more importantly, once the system was stable, it was actually really good. The search function alone - I can find any document in seconds now. Before, I might spend two or three minutes looking through folders.

The Epic integration, once it was working, is seamless. I can pull up a patient's records and immediately see all the linked documents. That's a big deal in emergency medicine.

And frankly, the way CloudSync handled the crisis showed me they're a real partner. Any vendor can be good when things go well. How they respond when things go wrong tells you who they really are.

---

[RESULTS AND BENEFITS - 28:00-42:30]

MARCUS: Now that you're six months post-implementation, what results are you seeing?

MICHAEL: The numbers speak for themselves. Our IT team is spending about 75% less time on file-related support tickets. We've decommissioned all six of our old file servers, which saves us real money in maintenance and power.

For compliance, what used to take our HIPAA officer 10-12 hours a week is now about 2 hours. The automated audit logging and reporting are exactly what we needed.

And the ROI - we did a formal analysis with our CFO. We're looking at about $760,000 in annual benefits against a $425,000 first-year investment. Payback was around six months.

MARCUS: Amanda, from a clinical perspective?

AMANDA: The impact on my department has been significant. Search time is down dramatically - we're talking seconds instead of minutes. That matters when you're juggling multiple patients.

The mobile access has been a game changer too. Physicians can pull up documents from anywhere in the hospital on their phones. Before, they had to find a workstation.

But the biggest thing? My staff actually likes the system now. That first weekend, I had nurses telling me they wanted to go back to paper. Six months later, they're telling me CloudSync is one of the best tools we've given them.

MARCUS: Any specific stories or examples you can share?

AMANDA: Last month, we had a complex trauma case - multiple victims from a car accident. In the past, coordinating documentation across trauma surgery, radiology, and pharmacy would have been chaotic. With CloudSync, everyone was accessing and updating the same documents in real-time. The trauma surgeon told me afterward it was the smoothest information flow he'd experienced.

MICHAEL: On the admin side, our recent Joint Commission survey was notable. The surveyors commented on how quickly we could produce documentation they requested. Our compliance team was pulling reports in minutes that would have taken hours before.

---

[LOOKING FORWARD - 42:30-48:00]

MARCUS: What's next for HealthFirst with CloudSync?

MICHAEL: We've already expanded. We added our satellite dental clinic - 200 more users - and we upgraded to the advanced analytics module for compliance reporting.

Next year, we're looking at rolling out to our affiliated urgent care centers. That would be another 400-500 users. The platform has proven itself, so expanding is an easy decision.

We're also interested in some of the AI features on your roadmap. The intelligent search and automated classification could be valuable for our clinical documentation.

AMANDA: From my side, I'm hoping we can do more with mobile. Getting the nursing staff on tablets for bedside documentation, maybe some of the AI-assisted search Michael mentioned. The platform gives us a foundation to build on.

---

[CLOSING - 48:00-52:15]

MARCUS: Any advice for other healthcare organizations considering a similar project?

MICHAEL: A few things. First, invest in the implementation planning. The delays we experienced were partly because we didn't fully assess our own environment upfront. Do your homework before you sign the contract.

Second, have realistic expectations. Any enterprise deployment is going to have bumps. What matters is having a vendor who responds appropriately when issues arise.

Third, involve your clinical leaders early. Amanda's input during the selection process helped us choose a solution that actually works for the people using it every day.

AMANDA: I'd add - don't let a rocky start derail you. If we had given up after that first weekend, we would have missed out on something that's genuinely improved how we work. Push through the hard parts. The results are worth it.

And pick a vendor who treats you like a partner, not just a customer. CloudSync's response to our issues demonstrated that they were invested in our success, not just in closing a deal.

MARCUS: Thank you both so much for your time and candor today.

MICHAEL: Happy to help. We're proud of what we've accomplished with CloudSync, and I hope our story helps other organizations.

AMANDA: Agreed. And Marcus - make sure you include the bumpy parts in the case study. The honest version is more helpful than a sanitized one.
